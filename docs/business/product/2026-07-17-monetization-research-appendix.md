# Founder Lifetime — Research Brief (agent output, 2026-07-17)

## Key benchmarks (all cited in brief)
- Lifetime multiple: folk wisdom 2-3x annual is LOW end; RevenueCat spread 2x-12x, recommends pricing just above expected LTV, A/B 5x/8x/12x. Examples: Calm $79/yr→$399 (5x); Jumpspeak 3.6x; Moonly 2.1x; Placify 3.3x; Waking Up 11.5x. Airbridge: 3-5x. Flighty: $59.99/yr → $299 lifetime (~5x). CARROT Weather: no lifetime (per-user data costs). Overcast: donation-ware patronage converted only 1.9% (2.6% with patron features) — "support the dev" framing doesn't convert. Sources: revenuecat.com/blog/growth/lifetime-subscriptions, airbridge.io, blog.funnelfox.com/app-pricing-models-guide, flighty.com/pricing, marco.org/2016/09/09/overcast-ads.
- Scarcity: real caps/deadlines carry 8-32% conversion lift (urgency studies); fake timers burn trust. Membership launch example: 180/200 founding spots filled at warm-audience launch; founding discount guidance 20-30% off future standard. Average LTD does ~100 sales, outstanding ~500 (Indie Hackers) → "first 100 founders" is honest and hittable.
- Founder badge: Discord Early Supporter badge = canonical permanent "believed first" mechanic; near-zero marginal cost with acct_ identity.
- Cannibalization: RevenueCat State of Subscription Apps — lifetime-dominant apps $0.19 vs $0.36 revenue-per-install at D14 vs yearly-dominant ($0.24 vs $0.46 at D60). ~15-20% of LTD buyers are warm leads who'd have converted to annual within 60 days (pull-forward). AppSumo: founder keeps ~30% of price vs 85-100% first-party (small business program). Risks: loyalists convert prematurely; price increases never touch lifetime holders.
- Mechanics: lifetime = non-consumable IAP, same "pro" entitlement as subs (RevenueCat RN SDK handles); no trials on non-consumables; must build "active sub + lifetime → prompt cancel" detection yourself; SwiftUI SubscriptionStoreView can't show one-time purchases (need custom/RevenueCat paywall); Family Sharing toggle is one-way — leave OFF for founder SKU (Flighty withdrew family lifetime). Anchoring: 3-option paywall (monthly decoy, annual pre-selected, lifetime ceiling) pushes annual selection to 69-74%; annual retains ~2.5x monthly at 12mo (44.1% vs 17.5%).
- Launch playbook: test via owned channels (TestFlight list = exactly this) before public paywall; founders routinely sell ~50 LTDs to ~1k-person list pre-launch; warm-list launches convert ~10x cold. Message value-first ("everything FTF ever ships, one price, locked forever + Founder badge"), dev-support second.

## Tactical takeaways (verbatim from agent)
1. Price at 3-5x intended annual, not 2-3x. If launch annual ~$20-25/yr → $79 founder lifetime (~3.5-4x). $49 only if annual ≤$15.
2. Cap honestly at a hittable number — "first 100 Founders"; publicly close it.
3. Non-consumable on same "pro" entitlement, Family Sharing OFF, no trial; build sub+lifetime cancel-prompt detection day one.
4. Lifetime = anchor, not business model; after founder window, remove or reprice to 5x+ so annual stays default.
5. Expect ~15-20% pull-forward cannibalization; the 3-5x multiple + 85% Apple small-biz cut is what makes it positive.
6. Ship permanent Founder badge + recognition wall; window closes forever.
7. Headline locked-in value, sub-line solo-dev support (Overcast 1.9% proves donation framing fails).
8. Run as TestFlight-exclusive window pre-launch; A/B $79 vs $59 = owned-channel price test that calibrates the public paywall.

Day 0 = 90% of trial starts, 44.5% of purchases. Multi-page onboarding paywalls +37% (12.41% vs 9.07%, Superwall 40M opens). Win-back within 10-15 min of cancel.
Apple: Small Business Program 15%; since May 1 2025 US apps may link to external (Stripe) checkout with NO Apple commission (guideline 3.1.1/3.1.3 change); Ninth Circuit affirmed contempt Dec 2025; SCOTUS granted cert Jun 30 2026, ruling ~Jun 2027 → architect entitlements payment-agnostic (one entitlements table fed by StoreKit + Stripe webhooks); cross-platform entitlement sharing (3.1.3(b)) independent of injunction.
Cases: V1 Sports — freemium→paid+trial = +90% revenue in 12 mo despite backlash. Dynasty Daddy — free core, Patreon for league-personalized computation.
Takeaways: $4.99/mo, hero SKU $34.99-39.99/yr (undercut Nerds); soft paywall w/ league metering (free = 1 league + core values; paid = multi-league, unlimited finder runs); never gate raw values; multi-page paywall end of onboarding after showing 2-3 real mutual-gain trades; 14-30d trial on annual only (monthly = trial-less decoy anchor); payment-agnostic entitlements day one; sell annual hardest May-Aug; instrument cancel flow (survey + immediate discount offer) pre-launch.

## SEASON PASS
Comps: Fantasy Footballers UDK $34.99 / UDK+ $59.99 as YEAR-LABELED one-time IAPs in-app, new SKU each season (the model to copy); ETR Draft Kit Pro $49.99→$54.99 YoY + early-bird $45.99; Dynasty Nerds Rookie Guide $19.99 standalone spring product; Fantasy Points $239-299 early-bird ratchet; MLS Season Pass $99/season RETIRED 2026 (folded into Apple TV+).
Mechanics: prefer NON-CONSUMABLE year-labeled SKU ("2026 Season Pass") over non-renewing subscription — Apple handles restore, no expiry cron; non-renewing subs leave expiry + cross-device delivery entirely to developer. Next season = new SKU = natural repurchase. Auto-renew annual fires during Feb-Mar trough = churn magnet. Small Business Program: $19.99 nets ~$17.
Economics: median annual first-renewal only 23-40% anyway, so voluntary re-buy with strong Aug trigger can match auto-renew; subscription fatigue: 41% consumers fatigued, household subs 4.1→2.8 YoY; 35% of apps blend subs + one-time. No vendor publishes repurchase rates (revealed preference only).
Promo pattern: discount EARLY (May-June early-bird ~$14.99), FULL price Jul-Labor Day at urgency peak (inverse of normal discounting — demand is deadline-driven); search peaks Aug. Returning-buyer discount year 2 substitutes for auto-renew retention. Trough: DLF-style Jan/Feb flash sale on Rookie Pass; ship offseason value.
Takeaways: two SKUs matching demand spikes — spring Rookie Pass ~$9.99 (Apr-May) + fall Season Pass ~$19.99, bundle/upgrade path ($5-10 upgrade, UDK→UDK+ pattern); 500 passes ≈ $8.5k/season net.

## ADS (HYBRID FREE TIER)
eCPMs US iOS (Appodeal Q4 2024/2025): banner $0.45 (tier-1 range $0.50-1.50), interstitial $14.32, rewarded $19.63 (AppLovin tier-1 rewarded $15-40); native ~$3. Web RPM: AdSense $3-10; Mediavine $15-40+; Raptive ~$47-57 (one publisher); minimums DROPPED Oct 2025 — Raptive 25k pageviews/mo, Mediavine Journey 10k sessions/mo.
Comps (verified by fetching HTML): KTC runs Raptive/AdThrive (not AdSense) + donation link; FantasyCalc also Raptive; Dynasty Daddy runs Freestar + $6/mo Patreon ad-free club (the exact hybrid pattern). No comp is mobile-app-first — mobile ad real estate in dynasty is uncontested.
ATT: sports = #1 opt-in vertical ~50% (Adjust Q2 2025; industry avg 35%); no-IDFA gap ~50% lower rewarded eCPM; AdMob serves non-personalized without consent → show prompt with pre-prompt explainer, nearly pure upside.
Stack: AdMob via react-native-google-mobile-ads (official Expo config plugin; requires dev build not Expo Go — fine w/ EAS; watch GitHub issue #820 Expo SDK 54 plugin bug, pin fixed version); skip mediation until volume (AppLovin MAX advantages at ~100k+ DAU); app-ads.txt on web domain; 1-2 day setup.
Rewarded in non-game apps works: Sworkit/Duolingo patterns; $8-20 eCPM iOS non-game; 95%+ completion; Tapjoy: rewarded viewers 53% D30 retention vs 12-13% baseline. Cap ~3/day, tie to want-moment ("watch → unlock extra deep scan"), never gate core flow; rewarded watchers = warmest premium leads.
Revenue math @ 1,000 DAU: banners ~$4-20/day + rewarded ~$1.50-4/day ≈ $150-700/mo (ARPDAU $0.005-0.02). Hundreds of DAU = tens of $/mo. Ads = coffee budget year one.
Hybrid: layering ads onto purchase model lifted IAP revenue ~30%, LTV ~33% (Adjust); rewarded-preview users convert to purchase at higher rates; >50% of top-grossing non-game apps run IAP+ads. Failure mode: ad load up + retention down = traded LTV for pennies.
Takeaways: AdMob only, one adaptive banner on high-dwell browse screens (NOT mid-calculator) + one rewarded placement; ATT prompt yes; copy Dynasty Daddy hybrid not KTC pure-ads; web AdSense day one → Mediavine Journey at 10k sessions → Raptive at 25k; skip interstitials/native; instrument ARPDAU + retention delta via user_events; if D7 dips, cut banners before rewarded.

# Referral / give-get growth loops — Research Brief (condensed, agent output 2026-07-17)

Benchmarks: Dropbox (500MB both sides) 100k→4M users in ~15 mo; referrals ~35% of daily signups at peak; 2.8M invites in one month; embedded as an onboarding step. Evernote: 10 pts × first 3 referrals (~3 Premium months) then 5 pts only on referred PURCHASES — cost control in the curve. Duolingo: 1 free Super week per friend, cap 10 weeks, both sides (modern conservative template). Spotify: free month both sides. Participation: 44% of consumers join referral programs; 60% of non-participants never got a link (distribution is the bottleneck). Mature programs = 10-30% of acquisition. Viral coefficient: K 0.15-0.25 good, 0.4 great, 0.7 outstanding, >1 rare/temporary. Wharton bank study: referred customers +16-25% CLV, -18% churn, net of reward cost. Sources: growsurf.com/blog/dropbox-referral-program, referralcandy.com/blog/evernote-referral-program, refer.codes/duolingo, impact.com/referral/top-10-referral-marketing-statistics, andrewchen.com/how-to-design-a-referral-program, faculty.wharton.upenn.edu Schmitt-Skiera-vandenBulte 2011.

Reward sizing: norm = 1 free month (or week for cheap subs) per successful referral, both sides, capped. Milestone variants (1 = trial extension, 5 = features, 10 = free year) have off-the-shelf templates (Viral Loops). Double-sided: 78%+ of programs, 65% of referrers prefer shared framing — but A/B tests sometimes favor inviter-weighted asymmetric offers; the "+45% dual-sided" figure is weakly sourced. Treat sidedness as an A/B question.

Network-shaped products: Sleeper >90% viral growth via league invites (a16z memo) — league creation forces ~11 invites; "the group structure WAS the referral program." Splitwise = canonical product-improving invite ("pull" virality — invite is a favor, not an ad; converts better than "push"). TRUE group-unlock ("all 12 get it when 8 join") has NO published case study — genuine white space; nearest documented = milestone-referral templates.

Apple compliance: guideline 3.2.2(x) — apps may incentivize in-app actions, but rewarding the INVITEE for download/signup has drawn rejections; tie every reward (both sides) to in-app action ("referred leaguemate completes N matchups"), never the install. 3.1.2(a): don't gate paid features behind invites. Granting: RevenueCat promotional entitlements (API-granted, time-boxed, never charges — docs name referral programs as the use case) or home-rolled server-side flag on acct_ (equally compliant). StoreKit offer codes = for win-back blasts, not automated per-event grants (can't be minted programmatically). App Review is inconsistent on referral mechanics — describe plainly in Notes for Review.

Fraud/failure: unprotected programs see 5-15% fraud (self-referral via secondary emails); controls = pending-until-activation rewards, per-referrer caps, fingerprinting. FTF's free control: only reward verified co-members of the referrer's real Sleeper league, one reward per unique Sleeper user ever. Programs flop from invisibility (buried in settings), weak sharing moments, unwanted rewards, redemption friction. Measure cost per INCREMENTAL customer (holdouts) before scaling reward size.

Sharing moments: Spotify Wrapped 60M+ shares, +21% downloads launch week — identity-flattering personal-data cards. Branch: contextual deep links / previews lift click-to-install 2-6x; user-to-user share links = highest-converting install channel. CTA placement: in main flows at value peaks (trade found, ranking done), native-styled, asked in many places — Dropbox made it an onboarding step.

Takeaways: league = referral unit, loop is pull not push; 1 Pro month per ACTIVATED leaguemate cap 4-6/season + milestone (8+ activated → free season pass); run the group-unlock A/B (white space); action-gate everything for Apple; grant server-side; share card ships before the referral program; instrument for K ≈ 0.2-0.5 and referred-user retention as the validation metric.

# Accuracy contests + creator-marketplace economics — Research Brief (condensed, agent output 2026-07-17)

FantasyPros accuracy mechanics: rank→implied-points ("Accuracy Gap" = |implied − actual|, summed, lower better); snapshot locks (draft = frozen at Wk1 kickoff; in-season = Thu + Sun 1pm snapshots, Thu players lock); relevance weighting 1.0 top slots → 0.5 deep (since 2021); QB/RB/WR/TE only; 16 of 17 weeks, worst week dropped from Wk8, weekly z-scoring; unranked players imputed at deepest-rank+1; ~150-200 experts. Experts join by application/partnership, paid in EXPOSURE not cash (badges, profile, promotion); weekly competition explicitly "no cash or prizes." Trust hook = My Playbook custom consensus (adopt/weight chosen experts across all tools).
Dynasty accuracy = WHITE SPACE: no published retrospective dynasty-rankings scoring exists anywhere. Proxies: future-value realization (multi-year implied-points), trade-value trajectory vs KTC/consensus 6-12 mo later (fast feedback), rookie-class hit rates (StatChasers outcome tiers), startup-ADP drift (DLF archives).
TipRanks: 3-factor (success rate, avg return, statistical significance w/ 10-15 call minimum); scarce star tiers; score timestamped calls not people. Metaculus: peer-relative scores (sum to zero per question — difficulty-immune), public track-record/calibration page, separate leaderboards + medals.
Design: score snapshots on multiple horizons (13wk/1yr/2yr) vs dual benchmark (production truth slow + market truth fast); peer-relative z-scores; min-sample gate before badges (≥2 scored windows); rolling 24-mo percentile badges w/ decay; publish methodology + track-record page day one; all batch-computable via quarterly cron.

Marketplace economics: take rates — Patreon 10%+processing (post-Aug-2025), Substack 10%, Gumroad 10%, YouTube 30%, Twitch 50% (70/30 first $100k Plus), OnlyFans 20%, Whop ~6-7% all-in (killed 30% commission May 2025), tipster marketplaces 50-70% platform take. Norm: platform take rises with how much discovery/trust/audience the platform owns → FTF (owns audience + computes trust) justifies ~30%.
Stripe Connect Express: $2/mo per active connected account + 0.25%+$0.25/payout; hosted KYC/W-9; 1099 e-file $2.99/form. OBBBA (Jul 2025): 1099-K back to $20k AND 200 txns; 1099-NEC $2k from TY2026 → hobbyist contributors trip almost nothing. Credits-only payouts avoid money transmission entirely (FinCEN closed-loop exemption <$2k/day); cashable credits = Roblox DevEx territory (third-party payout vendor). Dabble Copy Cash: ~$0.10 credit per copied entry = micro-reward precedent.
Apple: 3.1.1 — UGC digital content in-app must use IAP; credits/tips explicitly permitted; Patreon forced migration (deadline Nov 1 2026; creators choose raise-iOS-price-~43% or absorb; custom/dynamic pricing killed on iOS — pre-registered SKU problem). Workarounds: fixed price tiers OR consumable credit packs (dynamic pricing in your ledger — Apple-sanctioned). US-only: post-Epic web-checkout steering legal.
Precedents: nobody sells user-generated RANKINGS (white space); picks marketplaces thrive (Whop, Tipstrr, Dabble) — common thread: verified track record IS the product.
Takeaways: ship accuracy leaderboard 1-2 seasons before marketplace; credits on iOS (3-5 fixed SKUs), web checkout discount; 30% take on net; two-tier payout ladder (credits default → Stripe Express opt-in at $100 + verified tier); gate selling on measured accuracy not application; same scoring pipeline covers platforms and power users; adoption must re-weight the trade engine, not just display a list.
