Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 19, 2026

Fantasy Trade Finder ("we", "us", or "the Service") operates fantasy-trade-finder.onrender.com, the Fantasy Trade Finder mobile app, and a companion browser extension. Fantasy Trade Finder is a small, independent hobby project for dynasty fantasy football managers. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it, how long we keep it, and the choices you have.

1. Information we collect

  • Sleeper account and league data. You sign in with your Sleeper username only. We look up your public Sleeper profile (user ID, username, display name, avatar), your fantasy leagues for the current season, league rosters, draft-pick assets, and the usernames and display names of your leaguemates. All of this is read from Sleeper's public, read-only API — signing in never involves a password. Because username entry alone isn't authentication, one honest caveat: until you verify with your Sleeper login, anyone who knows your username could open a session that sees this board — verifying locks it to you.
  • Sign-in identity (Apple or Google). If you sign in with Apple (or, in the future, Google), we store the stable anonymous identifier the provider issues for our app (Apple/Google sub), the provider name, and — if the provider shares an email address — a one-way SHA-256 hash of it. We never store your email address itself, your name from the provider beyond an optional display name you can change, or any provider password. This identifier exists only to recognize your account across devices and sign-ins.
  • Your rankings and in-app activity. Every ranking comparison you make (three-player matchups, tier saves, manual reorders, pick-anchor answers), the per-player ratings computed from them, rating history over time, trade likes and passes, trade cards shown to you, mutual trade matches, "bad trade" flags, players you skip or mark untouchable/target, and your team-outlook and position preferences. We also record activity events (sign-in, ranking, trade actions) along with a device snapshot: device type, OS version, and app version.
  • Usage analytics (first-party only). To understand where the app is confusing or broken, the apps send us usage events: screens viewed, steps of sign-in and setup completed, taps on core features, how long actions take, and app errors (error messages are scrubbed of personal data and truncated before they leave your device). These events are processed and stored only on our own server — no analytics company receives them. Each install generates a random device identifier (a meaningless ID like dev_a1b2…, stored in your device's secure storage) so we can count installs and see where new users get stuck before they sign in; it contains no information about you or your device. If we run product experiments (showing different versions of a feature to different users), the variant you were shown is recorded with these events so we can tell whether the change helped. The event record also has room for a coarse two-letter country code (e.g. "US") supplied by a CDN header; we never derive it from your IP address ourselves, and because the service currently runs without such a CDN, this field is empty today. If we put a CDN in front of the service, usage events would start carrying that country-level (never more precise) code.
  • In-app feedback. If you submit feedback we store the note text, which screen it was filed from, a severity label, and device metadata (app version, platform, device type, OS version). Feedback can be submitted anonymously; if you're signed in it's attributed to your account. The mobile app also keeps a local copy on your device.
  • Push notification tokens. If you enable push notifications, we store an opaque Expo push token and your platform (iOS/Android) so we can deliver alerts, plus your notification preferences including your time zone (used for quiet hours).
  • Optional Sleeper connection token ("Send in Sleeper" beta). If you opt into sending trade proposals directly to Sleeper, you sign in to sleeper.com inside an in-app browser window. Your Sleeper password is entered on Sleeper's own site — we never see, receive, or store it. The app captures the session token Sleeper issues and stores it encrypted at rest (Fernet symmetric encryption; the key lives only in our server environment, never in the database, and the token is never logged or returned to any client). This token is a full-account Sleeper credential: we use it only to submit or withdraw the trade proposals you explicitly ask us to send. You can disconnect at any time in the app's Settings ("Disconnect Sleeper sending"), which deletes the stored token immediately; it also expires on Sleeper's schedule (about one year).
  • Crash and performance data (Sentry). The mobile app uses Sentry to report crashes and performance traces (a sample of app interactions). Reports include device model, OS version, and app version, and after sign-in are tagged with your pseudonymous Sleeper user ID only (never your username or any name) so we can investigate your bug reports; the tag is cleared when you sign out. Sentry is configured not to collect additional personal data by default.

2. Information we do NOT collect

  • No passwords. Sign-in is by Sleeper username via Sleeper's public API. The optional Sleeper connection captures a session token, never your password.
  • No email addresses, phone numbers, or payment information — the Service has no email field, no billing, and no in-app purchases.
  • No location, contacts, camera, microphone, or health data.
  • No advertising or third-party marketing analytics SDKs — there are no ad networks and no trackers such as Google Analytics, Meta, Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, or similar in the app or website. The usage analytics described above are collected and stored by us alone; the only third party receiving diagnostic data is Sentry, for crashes.
  • We do not sell your data, and we do not share it with advertisers or data brokers.

3. How we use your data

  • To run the product: build your rankings, generate trade suggestions, match you with leaguemates on mutually liked trades, show value trends, and send the notifications you opted into.
  • Rankings and trade discovery. Trade suggestions work by comparing your player values with your leaguemates' values to find trades both sides should like. Your raw ranking list is never displayed to other users, but it does influence which trade suggestions your leaguemates see. When you "like" a trade, the other manager may see an interest indicator on the mirrored trade, and a mutual like creates a match visible to both of you. Leaguemates can also see an aggregate "contrarian score" beside your username — a single number for how far your rankings deviate from the league consensus per position — never which players you rank high or low.
  • AI-assisted matchup selection. To pick informative ranking matchups, we may send player names, positions, and anonymous rating signals to Anthropic's Claude API. These requests contain no account identifiers — no username, user ID, or league name.
  • Sharing features. Share images contain only content you created and choose to share. If the public-profile feature is enabled, your profile page (at /u/your-username) would show your display name, avatar, league count, your saved tier snapshot, and your top over/under-consensus takes; it never exposes private league data or other users' rankings. This feature is currently off.
  • To debug and improve the Service (crash reports, feedback, activity events).

4. Third parties

  • Sleeper — we read your public account and league data from Sleeper's API; with your opt-in, we send your trade proposals to Sleeper. We are not affiliated with Sleeper.
  • DynastyProcess — we ingest their publicly published consensus player values to seed ratings. No data about you is sent to DynastyProcess.
  • Render — hosts our server and database. Your data is stored on Render's infrastructure, and Render keeps standard server request logs.
  • Expo and Apple/Google — push notifications are delivered through Expo's push service and the platform notification services (APNs / FCM), which process your push token and notification content.
  • Sentry — receives crash and performance reports from the mobile app as described above.
  • Anthropic — receives player names and anonymous ranking signals for matchup selection, as described above.
  • Google Fonts — our web pages load fonts from Google, which receives your IP address as part of serving the font files. The mobile app bundles its fonts and makes no such request.

5. Data retention

  • Account and league data (profile, leagues, rosters) is kept while your account is active so the Service works across sessions.
  • Rankings, rating history, and daily consensus value snapshots are retained indefinitely. This is deliberate: historical values power the trends, history charts, and season-recap features, and once a day passes those numbers cannot be reconstructed.
  • Ranking comparisons, trade decisions, activity and usage events are append-only logs kept for product function and quality analysis. On account deletion, the records keyed to your account are deleted outright; a small set of shared records is anonymized instead so the other manager's history stays intact — see section 6.
  • Feedback is kept through its triage lifecycle (new → planned/in progress → fixed/shipped or declined) and retained as a product record afterward.
  • Push tokens are kept until you sign out on that device, disable notifications, or your data is deleted.
  • Sleeper connection tokens are kept encrypted until you disconnect in the app (immediate deletion), the token expires (about one year), or your data is deleted.
  • Sign-in sessions are short-lived server records that expire automatically.
  • Server logs used for debugging are transient; our hosting provider retains standard request logs per its own policy.

6. Deletion and your choices

  • Delete your account: go to Settings → Delete account in the app. Deletion is immediate and self-serve — no waiting period and no support request needed. It removes your user record, rankings and rating history, ranking comparisons, trade decisions, preferences, notifications and push tokens, activity and usage events, and any stored Sleeper connection token. A small set of shared records is anonymized instead of erased so the other manager's history stays intact: trade matches keep the other side's record with your identifier replaced by a deleted-user placeholder, references to you on other users' trade decks and "bad trade" flags are cleared, and any feedback you submitted is kept as an anonymous product record. The app also clears its local analytics queue when your account is deleted. If you can't use the in-app path, you can still request deletion through the in-app feedback button or the support contact on our App Store listing.
  • Export your data: you can download a copy of the data we hold about you (profile, rankings, decisions, events) from the app's Settings; it's returned as a machine-readable file.
  • Device identifier after deletion: the random install identifier lives in your device's secure storage, which apps cannot fully erase; it persists on the device even after account deletion (and survives reinstalls). It links to nothing on our servers after anonymization — if you sign up again from the same device, our systems see a new user on a previously seen device, nothing more. Erasing your device removes it entirely.
  • Disconnect Sleeper sending: in the app's Settings at any time — the stored token is deleted immediately.
  • Push notifications: can be turned off per-category in Settings or entirely in your device settings.
  • Sign out: ends your session on that device and clears the Sentry account tag.

7. Security

Traffic to the Service uses HTTPS. Sleeper connection tokens are encrypted at rest with a key held only in the server environment. That said, this is a small beta service run as a hobby project — we take reasonable measures, but no method of storage or transmission is completely secure.

8. Children

The Service is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from them. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

9. Changes to this policy

We'll post updates on this page and change the effective date above. Material changes will also be announced in the app.

10. Contact

Questions or requests: use the in-app feedback button (Settings → feedback), or the support contact listed on our App Store page.
