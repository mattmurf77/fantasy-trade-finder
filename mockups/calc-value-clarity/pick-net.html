Variant C — Net-pick chip (currency framing)

Approach Lead with a single answer: one net pick chip — "+ a Mid 2nd → Side A" — treating the whole trade like change owed. Below it, each player is converted to its tier pick, raw value as a quiet subline. The rawest expression of "value in picks, not an arbitrage number."

Primary The net-pick chip = who wins + by how much, in one object. Secondary per-player pick conversions; raw values are the smallest text on screen.

Reuses gap.pick_equivalent (the net chip) + the tier ladder (per-player rows). No new math.

Tradeoff Most opinionated / least busy, but hides magnitude nuance — a 2-piece side still lists rows, and "net = one pick" can under-read a lopsided multi-player deal. Best when paired with Variant A's bar for users who want to see the tilt, not just the number.
