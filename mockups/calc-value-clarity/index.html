FB #157 — Make the calculator show WHO wins & by how many PICKS

Three mockups of the Trade Calculator verdict, dark Chalkline, ~390px. Same sample trade throughout: Side A sends Jahmyr Gibbs (1st, 7,000) for Side B's Malik Nabers (2 1sts, 8,500) — an 82% deal that Side A wins by ≈ a Mid 2nd.

Operator vision: "Hard to see what the value is here, and who's winning the deal. And would still love the value being based on picks rather than some arbitrage number value."

The mockups

A · Pick-denominated tilt bar Recommended

A tug-of-war bar whose full scale is ± a Mid 1st — the needle position literally reads in picks. Big "SIDE A WINS" headline + "≈ a Mid 2nd" margin pill. Directly answers FB-169's value-bar ask.

B · Two-package ledger + verdict banner

Each side is a column; every player wears its tier chip ("1st" vs "2 1sts"). Shows why one side is heavier. Colored banner states the winner in one pick sentence. Closest to today's card — lowest build risk.

C · Net-pick chip (currency framing)

Leads with a single object: "+ a Mid 2nd → Side A", the whole trade as change owed. Per-player tier conversions below, raw values as the quietest text. Most opinionated, least busy.

Recommendation

Ship Variant A (tilt bar) as the headline, borrow Variant B's per-player tier chips. A nails both operator asks in one glance: the ice needle answers "who wins," and because the bar's full scale is one first, its position is the pick margin — no arbitrage number needed. The "≈ a Mid 2nd" pill is the same gap.pick_equivalent the card already computes.

B's move worth stealing: tag each player chip with its tier label in pick terms (Gibbs "1st", Nabers "2 1sts"). That's the honest per-side pick denomination the operator wants, and it reuses the tier ladder already computed for every valued asset — no new math. Put those chips on the Side A / Side B builder rows, keep the tilt bar as the verdict.

C is the cleanest single idea but too lossy alone — keep the net-pick chip in reserve as a compact/share layout.

Everything reused, zero new engine work: give_value, receive_value, point_ratio, verdict, favors, gap.firsts, gap.pick_equivalent, and the per-player tier from the ladder — all already on /api/trade/evaluate and the values feed.

What we honestly DON'T have (don't fake it)

  • No summed "package = N picks" figure. The engine denominates individual players in tiers and the gap in one pick — it never adds players into a single pick sum. All three mockups respect that: per-player tier chips + a one-pick margin, never "this package = 2 firsts + a 2nd."
  • Live mode is consensus-only. "Who wins" here is by market value, not roster fit. The two-board "who wins by whose board" read only exists in In league mode (InLeagueCalculator) — these verdict treatments should carry over there, labelled as such.
  • No redraft values. Dynasty only; the redraft basis is reserved but 501s ("soon"). Don't imply a redraft winner.
  • Gaps bigger than one first have no single-pick equivalent — fall back to the existing "≈ 1.4 mid 1sts" copy (tilt bar pins the needle to the end).
