Avatar Lab · Guided Onboarding Experiment

Pick your Assistant GM

Six Clippy-school characters — oversized eyes, heavy brows, simple shapes — each shown doing the actual job: introducing itself and directing the user's next tap. The winner gets pose variants (pointing, celebrating, thinking), a dialogue engine, and a spotlight overlay, shipped as a new variant arm of the onboarding experiment.

Comments persist in this browser (localStorage). "Export notes" copies everything as JSON to paste back to Claude.

Coach: "Hey — I'm Coach, your assistant GM. I scout trades your league actually wants. Tap that first card and let's work."
CoachHuman · Assistant GM

The literal assistant GM — headset, team cap, credential lanyard. Reads as staff, not mascot; strongest fit if the avatar later fronts an AI chat assistant. Least whimsical of the six.

Pigskin: "I'm Pigskin — I live for this. See that trade? Swipe right if you'd take it. I'll learn your taste as you go."
PigskinTalking Football

The purest Clippy translation — the product's own object with a face. Instantly legible at 60px, animates well (wobble, spiral spin celebration), zero explanation needed.

The Agent: "Name's Agent. I move players. You want value? Build your board — one position, two minutes. I'll handle the calls."
The AgentFootball · In a Suit

Pigskin's older brother who went to business school. The suit + phone sells "I negotiate for you" — thematically perfect for a TRADE app, and the half-lidded eyes give it attitude the others don't have.

Whistler: "TWEET! I'm Whistler. My job: keep your trades fair. That card up top? Consensus values. Let's make them YOURS."
WhistlerReferee Whistle

The fairness mascot — a natural voice for the both-sides-win pitch and the "Bad trade?" flag. The TWEET verbal tic is a memorable attention-getter for step transitions, or an annoying one. High risk, high charm.

Blitz: "BLITZ here. No meetings, no fluff. Three swipes and I'll know your team better than your co-GM does. GO GO GO."
BlitzFootball Helmet

Ice-cyan shell puts the brand color ON the character. High-energy drill-sergeant persona suits impatient power users; may read aggressive for a first-run guide. Eyes-in-the-helmet is the strongest silhouette after Pigskin.

Playbook: "I'm Playbook — every trade in your league is diagrammed in here. First play: tell me who's untouchable on your roster."
PlaybookClipboard of Plays

The strategist — its face lives on the paper and its "body language" is X's-and-O's that can redraw per step (the play diagram becomes the tutorial pointer). Most flexible canvas for step-by-step guidance.

Mainframe: "SYSTEM ONLINE. I've already run 4,096 trade simulations for your roster. Shall I display the three your league would accept?"
MainframeSupercomputer

The war-room machine — face glows on a CRT, LEDs blink while it "computes." Leans hardest into raw analytical power ("4,096 simulations"); coldest personality of the set unless the copy keeps it warm.

Circuit: "BEEP. Hi! I'm Circuit. I crunch every roster in your league so you don't have to. First task: swipe on three trades. EASY."
CircuitRobot · Jersey 01

The friendly droid teammate — robot head, team jersey, ball tucked like a running back. Most kid-friendly of the AI set; the BEEP verbal tic and LED antenna give cheap, charming animation hooks.

Cortex: "Fascinating roster. I'm Cortex — I think about dynasty value so hard my stitches hurt. Your WR room is inefficient. Shall we fix it?"
CortexBrainiac · Laced Brain

A brain with football stitching — the smartest-guy-in-the-league-chat, wire-rim glasses and all. The visual pun (brain + ball are the same organ here) is the cleverest of the set; slightly gross to some audiences.

Quant: "Hi — Quant, lead scientist, Trade Lab. Hypothesis: your team improves in two moves. Care to review the evidence?"
QuantData Scientist

Lab coat, chaos hair, a tablet with the line always going up. Frames every onboarding step as an experiment ("hypothesis → evidence"), which is honest to how the product actually works under the hood.

The Analyst: "Per my model — hi, I'm The Analyst — your RB2 is overvalued by 14%. I've drafted three sell-high packages. Swipe when ready."
The AnalystData Analyst · Football Guy

Pigskin's spreadsheet-brained cousin — glasses, laptop of bar charts, speaks in percentages. The literal fusion you asked for: football body, analyst soul. "Per my model" is a ready-made catchphrase.
