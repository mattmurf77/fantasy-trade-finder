Variant B — Launcher Hub + Focused Modes

Structure: The Trades home is a hub: a "Trade DNA" summary panel at the top (your live prefs at a glance) plus four launcher cards, one per mode, each showing a one-line description and its current relevant selection. Tapping a card opens that mode full-screen. Every focused mode carries a lateral quick-switch chip row so you jump between modes without returning to the hub.

What's shown

  • Hub with Trade DNA panel + 4 launcher cards (tap a card to open its mode; the demo opens Specific Player).
  • Specific Player is the NBA2K centerpiece: full-width two-column FOR / AWAY board + a "Both directions" toggle and a big Build CTA.
  • Quick-switch chips (Guided · Team · Player · Calc) at the top of every focused mode; "Trade DNA" edits from the hub or the "Edit prefs" link.

Tradeoffs

  • + Breathing room — each mode gets a full screen, so the NBA2K board and Calculator aren't cramped.
  • + Trade DNA panel makes global prefs the first thing you see; recommendation chips are surfaced on the hub.
  • − One extra tap to reach a mode (mitigated by lateral switch chips once inside).
  • − Two homes for prefs (DNA panel summary + inside each mode) risks feeling like two places to edit if not disciplined.

Reuse vs. new

  • Reuse: Specific Player board = FB-47 finder targeting (pinned_give/receive). Calculator = TradeCalculatorScreen. Untouchables = asset_prefs #95. Positions in DNA = OutlookSheet chips. Card-launcher pattern mirrors RankHomeScreen's method chooser.
  • New: the hub shell + Trade DNA summary panel; "Specific Team" mode; per-card state summaries. Recommendation chips: roster needs/surplus already computed (analyze_roster_strengths) but not yet exposed to clients — needs a small endpoint.
