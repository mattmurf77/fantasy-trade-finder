3 · Rail — vertical team tag, age in the far corner

  • Chip: ≈117×48px unchanged — pitch stays 52px, ≈9 rows / 27 chips per screenful. The most compact option that adds both fields.
  • Team: a 14px position-colored left rail (WR #3B82F6) carrying the abbrev vertically — 8px Plex Mono, ink-0 text (contrast ≈5.6:1 on WR blue; ≈8:1 on QB orange). Age: 9px Plex Mono chalk-dim at the right end of the meta line — the corner opposite the check / rank badge, so the two never collide.
  • Name run: ≈99px → ≈87px (−12px ≈ 2 chars at 12px) — the rail + its 6px gap replace the old 8px left padding. “Jaxon Smith-Njigba”-class names ellipsize one word-part sooner than today; everything up to ≈13 chars still fits clean. Tier label unchanged on the meta line.
  • Dropped: the on-chip position text — the rail's color plus the active tab's “WR” carry it. Flagged honestly against the accessibility floor (“position color never the only encoding”): on the chip itself the rail IS color-only; its text is the TEAM, not the position. The pairing survives only via the tab, one level up.
  • States rendered: 5 selected (ice border + check); Quick Rank strip below the frame shows the rank-badge state (badge top-right, age still bottom-right — no collision by construction).
  • Honest tradeoffs: 8px vertical type is at the legibility floor — readable held close, slow to scan across a 27-chip grid, and rotated Latin text is unusual in this design language (nothing else in Chalkline sets type vertically). The rail also makes the position color do double duty (encoding + chip edge), and 2-char teams (“NO”, “KC”) leave the rail half-empty while “WAS” fills it.
